Bug Out Bag

  • Medical consent and history forms for each family member
  • passport, SSN & birth certificates
  • flashlight (batteries) and lighter/matches
  • notepad and pen
  • cell phone charger (solar charger if possible)
  • sunscreen & bug spray
  • whistle & glow stick
  • walkie talkies (batteries)
  • thermal blanket
  • first aid kit
  • Potassium iodide tablets
  • Binoculars
  • Tools (Multitool, assorted tools, knife)
  • weapon you know how to use
  • Food and Water
  • One change of clothes
  • Pot (for cooking and boiling water)
  • Plates and eating utensils (mess kit)
  • Duct tape
  • Water Filter or Water Purifier
  • Rope or Twine
  • Fire either propane/butane torches or camp stove
  • Sun glasses
  • Axe, Hatchet or Crowbar
  • Towels
  • Super glue
  • toilet paper, menstrual products
  • soap, shampoo, tooth brush and tooth paste

Camping Bag

it can be good for each member of the house hold to have their own personal bug out bag. It is good to have a single bag that contains all of the bulkier camping gear.

  • Tent
  • Sleeping Bags
  • Pillows

First Aid kit continued. Make sure to check it regularly. Any ointments and pills are likely to expire.

  • Adhesive bandages, triangular bandages, roller bandages, sterile gauze pads, and adhesive cloth tape
  • Medical tape
  • Ibuprofen (non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID))
  • Acetaminophen/paracetamol (over-the-counter pain relief)
  • Aspirin (over-the-counter pain relief)
  • Laxative
  • Iodine
  • Hand sanitizing liquid
  • Tweezers
  • Safety pins
  • Thermometer
  • Toothpicks/needles.
  • Absorbent compress dressings and an instant cold compress
  • Antibiotic ointment packets, hydrocortisone ointment packets, antiseptic wipe packets
  • A pair of non-latex gloves, scissors and a non-glass, non-mercury oral thermometer
  • Personal and prescription medications
  • A first aid instruction booklet and a list of emergency phone numbers, including contact information for your doctor, local emergency services, emergency road service providers, and the poison help line.
  • Multivitamins (to keep up health)
